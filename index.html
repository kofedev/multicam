MultiCam Simulator | Prototype

Problem Statement

Multicamera production is now used not only in studios, but also by streamers and content creators. Some media school and universities are beginning to include this topic in media and communication programs.

However, learning multicamera workflows can be difficult. Many students lack access to professional equipment or studio facilities, especially in remote or hybrid formats.

To address this challenge, we have been developing a prototype of a simple simulator using Unity 3D. It will help students and educators understand the basics of live multicamera production in different formats. The current prototype focuses on a music show setup and serves as a proof of concept. This is a non-commercial, research-oriented project, still in development.

Features

The prototype allows users to simulate the role of a director operating a video switcher during the filming of a 10-camera concert setup (including a crane and a dolly with two cameras).

The simulator workspace resembles a real TV studio:

  • A wall of monitors showing feeds from all 10 cameras, as well as a Preview Monitor and a Program Monitor.
  • A video switcher console.

    • The mixing console is a simplified video switcher consisting of two horizontal rows of buttons. Each button corresponds to a specific camera, with camera numbers displayed between the rows. One row is in Preview mode (lit green), indicating the source queued for broadcast, and the other is in Program mode (lit red), showing the source currently on air.

    When performing a Cut transition (by pressing the Enter key or clicking on the virtual T-Bar), the logical roles of the two rows are swapped: the source selected in Preview becomes the active Program output, and the previous Program source moves to Preview. The physical positions of the rows remain unchanged, only their roles and button colors update accordingly.

    This behavior accurately reflects standard operations on professional broadcast video switchers.

    Controls

    The process can be operated either via keyboard or with mouse functions.

    Keyboard Controls

    Keys 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 0 – switch the corresponding camera live (key “0” selects camera 10) the feed from that camera will appear on the Program monitor.

    Key combinations RightShift + 1, RightShift + 2, …, RightShift + 0 – send the corresponding camera to preview (the feed from that camera will appear on the Preview monitor).

    Enter – performs a Cut, i.e., switches the camera currently in Preview to the Program.

    Spacebar – pauses the simulation.

    Esc – returns to the start scene.

    Mouse Controls

    By clicking on any of the control monitors linked to the cameras, the corresponding camera feed is sent to the Program. Clicking the T-Bar performs a Cut, switching the selected Preview source to Program.

    Planned Improvements

    Work on the project is ongoing: transition effects, more realistic textures, additional levels (covering various genres beyond music shows), and control of individual cameras.
